The PEAS framework: Policy, Ecology, Agriculture, and Social science.

Policy

National goals revolutionize ecology on farms

The Obama administration set ambitious national goals for honey bees, pollinator habitat, and monarch butterflies. These policies altered the direction of pollinator research in the United States, and increased funding for farmers to conserve pollinators.

Check out my policy synthesis with Michigan State University researchers

  • Responding to the US national pollinator plan: a case study in Michigan. Link to paper

Setting goals for organic agriculture in the US

My prior research showed goal setting creates targets for researchers and farmers. PolicyNow seeks to determine the ecological, economic, and societal benefits of national goals for organic agriculture. These goals are based on the EU Farm to Farm Strategy, which set the goal of having 30% of land in agricultural production by 2030.

PolicyNow

  • National analysis of organic agriculture benefits
  • Determine farmer policy preferences
  • Ground truthing of analysis predictions
Ecology

Biodiversity ecosystem function relationships

Theory predicts insect biodiversity increases ecosystem services, making insect conservation key for ecologically functional farms. Biodiversity theory has mechanistic underpinnings giving farmers new targets to promote ecosystem services.

Check out my research with Washington State University researchers on biodiversity theory

  • A novel application of the Price equation reveals that landscape diversity promotes the response of bees to regionally rare plant species. Link to paper
  • Long-term organic farming and floral diversity promotes stability of bee communities in agroecosystems. Link to paper

Connecting beta diversity with community assembly

Approaches that track changes in the mechanisms of community assembly across farming systems are underdeveloped; niche and stochastic processes assess community asssembly, while beta diversity examines changes in species composition. Contecting these processes will create a mechanistic form of beta diversity that is linked with biodiversity ecosystem function theory.

Project BetaFun

  • Use simulations to recover community assembly mechanisms in beta diversity
  • Apply to existing datasets to uncover hidden mechanisms
  • Integrate into decision support tools for mechanistic decision support
Agriculture

Decision aid systems for farmers

Spatio-temporal variation in local farming practices and landscape level features shape biodiversity ecosystem function relationships. Decision support systems give land managers new tools to promote the economic, ecological, and social sustainability of their farms.

Check out my papers on text message based decision support and spatio-temporal variation

  • A novel plant pathogen management tool for vector management. Link to paper
  • A newly invasive species may promote dissimilarity of pest populations between organic and conventional farming systems. Link to paper

Ecosystem service decision support tools

My research indicates that farmers need context dependent, local, rapid decision support that integrates with their farming system. At present, no system exists that allows farmers to select from management options that promote or improve their ecosystem services over time.

EcoHero

  • Develop heuristical synthetic decision support system for ecosystem services
  • Establish a high throughput ecosystem service monitoring facility
  • Enhance model performance with ground truthing along orthogonal gradients
